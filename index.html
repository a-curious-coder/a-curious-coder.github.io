Rubik's Cube Solver

For my dissertation, I created a Rubik's Cube Solver using Processing-Java. This involved visualizing the cube in 2D and 3D formats. I developed and applied various search algorithms with some clever heuristics and generated graphs based on the solving results. My Rubik's Cube Solver can solve the traditional and pocket cube in 5 different ways. My next goal is to cater the solver for larger cube sizes, using the 'Reduction Method'.

Invoice Generator

I contacted a local dairy business to offer up my services for their dairy business. I created an invoice generator that allows the employees of the company to generate an invoice based on the delivery records. The invoice can be printed and emailed to the customer. Moreover, I've also created a dashboard visualising the important statistics of their company. I hope to implement a shortest-path algorithm to optimise time spent travelling on their delivery routes.

Crypto Analyst

Inspired by a Blockchain module I studied in my BSc degree, I decided to pursue a time-forecast regression model to predict the future prices of any given crypto-currency. This uses a LSTM network which was the best approach for the problem.

Parkinsons Classifier

Predicts whether a patient has Parkinson's Disease or is Healthy. There are various classifiers used here within the machine learning and deep learning domains. The model yielding the best results is Random Forest since the public data-set used is so small that deep learning as an approach is overkill; thus the Random Forest is the popular and more successful approach.
